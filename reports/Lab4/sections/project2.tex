\subsection{Linearity and Error Analysis}
From the data, the full-scale voltage ($V_{FS}$) is approximately $1288.70$ mV. 
The ideal step for the 5-bit increments (which corresponds to a digital step of 8 in an 8-bit scale) is calculated as:
\begin{equation}
    \Delta V_{step} = \frac{V_{max} - V_{min}}{31} = \frac{1288.70 - 0}{31} \approx 41.57 \text{ mV}
\end{equation}

The actual LSB of the 8-bit representation is:
\begin{equation}
    V_{LSB\_8bit} = \frac{\Delta V_{step}}{8} \approx 5.19 \text{ mV}
\end{equation}

Comparing the measured step ($\approx 41.6$ mV) with the theoretical one, the DAC shows an extremely high degree of linearity. The maximum deviation from the linear fit is minimal, indicating low Integral Non-Linearity (INL).
